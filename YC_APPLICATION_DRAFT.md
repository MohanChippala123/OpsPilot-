## Company

OpsPilot

## What are you building?

OpsPilot is an AI operations assistant for small service businesses. It gives owners and front desk teams one workspace to handle customer messages, draft replies, schedule appointments, create reminders, and track jobs.

## What problem are you solving?

Local service businesses run on messy inbound communication. Customers text, email, call, ask to reschedule, complain, request quotes, and need reminders. Owners either do this themselves after hours or hire admin help before they can afford it. OpsPilot reduces that operational drag by turning customer intake into structured workflows.

## Who are your users?

The first users are small service businesses with high inbound message volume and limited admin staff: HVAC companies, cleaning services, property managers, dental offices, contractors, and other appointment-based local operators.

## Why now?

Customers expect fast replies, but small businesses are still operating from phones, inboxes, calendars, and spreadsheets. LLMs make it possible for a small team to have an affordable AI operations layer that drafts responses and structures work without requiring a custom enterprise implementation.

## What is your insight?

The wedge is not "AI chatbot for every business." The wedge is the front desk workflow: classify the message, draft the reply, create the follow-up task, and keep the owner in control. Service businesses need supervised automation more than fully autonomous bots.

## What have you built?

An installable PWA that supports signup/login, dashboard metrics, customer message intake, AI-style reply suggestions, approval flow, calendar appointments, job tracking, business settings, offline-capable app install files, and a Vercel deploy path.

## What is your current status?

Public preview. The current free deployment stores workspace data locally in each user's browser so the product can be tested without paid infrastructure. The next production step is cloud Postgres/Supabase, hardened authentication, billing, and real LLM integrations.

## How will you get users?

Start manually with local service operators and founder-led outreach. The first channel is direct DMs, Instagram build-in-public content, local business communities, and conversations with operators in HVAC, cleaning, property management, and contracting. The goal is to get 5-10 operators using the workflow and measure which tasks they repeat.

## Competitors

Generic CRMs, scheduling tools, shared inboxes, and AI chatbot products. OpsPilot is focused on the service-business front desk workflow instead of being a broad CRM or a website chatbot.
